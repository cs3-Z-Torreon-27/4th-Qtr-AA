ASTEROIDS

Mostly located in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, asteroids are tiny, stony objects that orbit the Sun. They differ greatly in size and comparison, and some have the potential to threaten Earth by impact. Click here to learn more about their astrogeology!

PLANETS

Earth is one of the eight planets in our solar system, which are big celestial bodies that orbit stars. From gas giants like Jupiter and Saturn to rocky terrestrial planets like Mercury and Mars, they are all different in size and have different compositions and qualities. Click here to learn more about their astrogeology!

MOONS

Moons are naturally occurring satellites of planets that range in size from tiny rocky entities to massive icy worlds. They have a significant impact on how planetary habitats are shaped, possibly even supporting life through regulating tides. Click here to learn more about their astrogeology!
